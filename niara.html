📐 Psychometric Threshold Reference — How NIARA Categorizes Items

NIARA applies established psychometric criteria from classical test theory to classify each exam item. These thresholds are derived from the educational measurement literature including McDonald (1999), Haladyna & Rodriguez (2013), and NCSBN item development standards. All calculations are performed locally in your browser; no student data is transmitted.

✓ KEEPItem is performing within acceptable psychometric parameters. No action required. Statistics support retention without modification.
👁 REVIEWItem warrants closer inspection. One or more statistics approach borderline thresholds. Review in context of overall exam performance before deciding.
✏ REVISEItem shows measurable psychometric weakness. Revision of the stem, answer choices, or key is recommended before next administration.
🔴 REWRITEItem is functioning poorly. Negative discrimination or keying errors likely. Consider double-keying for the current exam and complete rewrite for the next.
📈 Point-Biserial Correlation (rpb)
r < 0.00REWRITENegative discrimination — higher performers chose wrong answer
0.00 ≤ r < 0.15REVISEVery low discrimination — item not meaningfully differentiating students
0.15 ≤ r < 0.20REVIEWMarginal discrimination — acceptable but borderline
r ≥ 0.20KEEP (if other criteria met)Acceptable to strong discrimination
The point-biserial correlation measures how well performance on a single item predicts overall exam performance. A value ≥ 0.20 is generally considered acceptable. Values ≥ 0.30 indicate strong discrimination.
📉 Item Difficulty (p-value)
p < 0.40REVISEFewer than 40% correct — item may be too difficult or flawed
0.40 ≤ p < 0.50REVIEWModerately difficult — verify appropriateness for cognitive level
0.50 ≤ p ≤ 0.95KEEP (if other criteria met)Acceptable range for nursing licensure-style items
p > 0.95REVIEWNear-ceiling — little contribution to score variance
The difficulty index (p) is the proportion of students who answered correctly. Ideal range for discriminating items is 0.50–0.85. Items < 0.40 may be overly difficult or contain flaws.
🔀 Discrimination Index (D)
D < 0.00REWRITENegative — lower performers outperformed upper group on this item
D ≥ 0.00AcceptableCalculated as upper 27% mean minus lower 27% mean item scores
The discrimination index compares item performance between the top and bottom 27% of scorers. A positive value confirms that higher-overall performers also performed better on this specific item.
⚠ Distractor Concern & Sample Size
Top wrong answer > 40%REVISEOne distractor is selected by a disproportionate number of students
n < 15 studentsREVISESample size too small for stable estimates — monitor over time
When a single incorrect distractor is selected by more than 40% of students, it may indicate an ambiguous stem, implausible correct answer, or a distractor that is more attractive than intended. Sample sizes below 15 produce unreliable statistics and should be interpreted cautiously.
References & Standards Haladyna, T. M., & Rodriguez, M. C. (2013). Developing and validating test items. Routledge.  |  McDonald, M. E. (1999). Systematic assessment of learning outcomes: Developing multiple-choice exams. Jones & Bartlett.  |  NCSBN Next Generation NCLEX item development standards (2023).  |  Ebel, R. L., & Frisbie, D. A. (1991). Essentials of educational measurement (5th ed.). Prentice-Hall.

All thresholds reflect standards commonly applied in nursing education psychometrics and are intended to guide, not replace, faculty professional judgment. Items flagged by NIARA should be reviewed in context of course content, student population, and curricular goals before final decisions are made. For items flagged REWRITE with a negative point-biserial, faculty should consider double-keying (awarding credit for the most-chosen incorrect answer) for the current exam administration while revising the item for future use.
